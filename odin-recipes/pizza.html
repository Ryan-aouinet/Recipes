Description

Authentic Napoletano Pizza Is a culinary masterpiece with a few key characteristics:   Simple, High-Quality Ingredients: It's made with just a few fresh ingredients: flour, water, yeast, salt, San Marzano tomatoes, fresh mozzarella, fresh basil, and olive oil. Unique Dough: The dough is made with a special type of flour ("00" flour) and is often left to ferment for a long time, resulting in a soft, chewy crust with a slightly crispy exterior.   Hand-Stretched: The dough is stretched by hand, not rolled, which helps to preserve the air in the dough and create a light and airy crust.   Wood-Fired Oven: It's cooked in a wood-fired oven at a very high temperature (around 900°F) for a very short time (60-90 seconds). This gives the pizza its signature charred spots and slightly smoky flavor.   Thin Center, Puffy Crust: The center of the pizza is thin and soft, while the edge (cornicione) is puffy and slightly chewy.   Wet in the Middle: Due to the thin center and fresh ingredients, Neapolitan pizza is often a bit wet in the middle. This is part of its charm and is why it's traditionally eaten with a fork and knife.   Classic Toppings: The most traditional toppings are tomato, mozzarella, basil, and olive oil (Margherita) or just tomato, garlic, oregano, and olive oil (Marinara).   Overall, authentic Neapolitan pizza is a delicious and unique culinary experience that's all about the quality of the ingredients and the traditional cooking methods.

Step by step Instructions

  1. Activate the Yeast: In a small bowl, combine the lukewarm water and yeast. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until foamy.
  2. Mix the Dough: In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Pour the yeast mixture into the flour and mix with a spoon or your hands until a shaggy dough forms.
  3. Knead the Dough: Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for 10-15 minutes. The dough should become smooth and elastic. If using a stand mixer, knead with the dough hook for about 8-10 minutes.
  4. First Rise (Bulk Fermentation): Lightly oil a large bowl. Place the dough in the bowl, turning to coat. Cover with plastic wrap or a damp cloth and let it rise in a warm place for 2-4 hours, or until doubled in size. This is called bulk fermentation and is crucial for flavor development.
  5. Divide and Shape the Dough: Once the dough has risen, punch it down gently and divide it into 2-3 equal portions. Gently shape each portion into a ball.
  6. Second Rise (Proofing): Place the dough balls on a lightly floured surface or in individual proofing containers. Cover and let them rise for another 1-2 hours. This second rise is called proofing and allows the dough to further develop its flavor and texture.
  7. Prepare the Toppings: While the dough is proofing, prepare your toppings. Crush or puree the San Marzano tomatoes and season with a pinch of salt. Slice the mozzarella into thin pieces or tear it into chunks. Prepare your pepperoni.
  8. Preheat Your Oven: Preheat your oven to the highest temperature possible, ideally 450-500°F (232-260°C) or higher. If you have a pizza stone or steel, place it in the oven while preheating. A wood-fired oven is ideal for Neapolitan pizza, but a regular oven can work too.
  9. Shape the Pizza: On a lightly floured surface, gently stretch or pat out one dough ball into a round shape, leaving a slightly thicker edge (cornicione). Avoid using a rolling pin, as it can deflate the dough.
  10. Top the Pizza: Spoon a thin layer of tomato sauce onto the pizza base, leaving a small border. Add the mozzarella, pepperoni (if using), and a few fresh basil leaves.
  11. Bake the Pizza: Transfer the pizza to the hot pizza stone or baking sheet in your oven. If using a pizza peel, dust it with flour or semolina to prevent sticking. Bake for 5-10 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is melted and bubbly. Keep a close eye on it as it cooks quickly at high temperatures.
  12. Finish and Serve: Remove the pizza from the oven and drizzle with extra virgin olive oil. Add a few more fresh basil leaves. Slice and serve immediately.
